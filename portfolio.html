4ce

Curated Mirco-Workout Wearable-Linked Website & App

Problem: The majority of American women living in poverty are clinically overweight. Many lack the monetary and educational resources necessary to maintain a gym membership, purchase workout equipment, or keep up to date with the best health and wellness practices. Solution: An app that sends women curated micro-workouts tailored to their fitness level and wellness goals an timed intervals throughout the day. Each workout is based on state-of-the-art HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training) research and aims to raise women’s basal metabolic rate through appropriately spaced workouts lasting no more than 4 minutes. The workouts are composed of natural body resistance exercises that can be done anywhere with no equiptment. To increase make 4CE (pronounced “force”) more fun to use, and thus increase user retention & the likelihood users will meet their fitness goals, we incorporated gasification aspects such as a leaderboard, stat tracking, and a time-sensitive point system algorithm for completing tasks. As as additional feature (one’s score is not negatively impacted by not using this feature) 4CE may be linked to one’s Jawbone wearable, to more specifically track progress. 4CE was born at Code 4 Health, a hackathon co-sponsored by The Clinton Foundation, Jawbone, Self magazine and Ace Hotels. 4CE Presentation (link) 4CE.xyz +Waterfall project management picture #Wearables #Biohacking #SocialJustice #Women #ClintonFoundation #AceHotel #Jawbone #Gamification #Fitness

Savatron

Children's Fiscal Responsibility Toy & App

Problem: How do we encourage fiscal responsibility in children under 12? A physical product & software idea similar to a Tamagotchi. Our toy is a small oval that clips onto children’s backpacks. The home screen starts with an egg that hatches into one of many characters depending on the children’s savings category. The character is linked to the child’s saving account and grows as the child makes deposits. The parents & child can set incremental savings goals. As these are met, the character “levels up”, gaining new features or powers. The tool is social, and children can battle other children’s characters. Savatron was concieved at a UX summit sponsored by Idean, a Finnish creative agency. #Idean #FiscalResponsibility #Gamification #KidsTech #BrainHacking

GIFSOME

Roles: Concept, Wireframe & Design

Animated GIFs are taking the internet by storm and I was frustrated with the inability to easily send them through iMessages on my iPhone. The process is sometimes mysterious and lengthy and near impossible for your less tech-savvy friends. I wanted an easier way. Bouncing ideas off of a co-worker at lunch, I wire-framed Gifsome on a napkin and started design shortly after. The app would tie in with Giphy's api and use the same organization that they'd come up with. The app would be simple, click and hold to preview animation and a single tap would copy the image to your clipboard so you can paste it in a message quickly.

MAKE IT

FRANKLIN POSTERS

Roles: Concept & Design

The concept for these posters was to visualize our companies core values. My initial idea was to create posters that incorporated different historical figures or characters from pop-culture to visually explain these values. Starting with the value "Make it better" and having read Benjamin Franklin's auto-biography the year prior, I knew he was the perfect representation of this value. During his life he was an apprentice printer, a statesman, scientist, inventor, postmaster, & swim instructor, just to name a few. In everything he did, he continually worked to make it better in one way or another.

Heinz Redesign

Roles: Wireframe & Design

While at the agency Biggs | Gilmore (now VML) I was tasked with redesigning the Heinz 57 homepage. Given requirements and hierarchy by a UX team member, I started sketching out my own wireframes. One of the client goals was to include push their featured recipes using vivd imagery. Running with this idea I designed a page that would envelope the user in an 'experience'.
