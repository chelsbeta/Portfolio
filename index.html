Philosophy

I seamlessly blend hip-hop lyrics into everyday conversation, and cite my sources. I feed off of high energy collaboration and prefer face-to-face conversation over phone calls. KELLOGG CAREERS

Roles: Concept, Wireframe & Design

While at Biggs | Gilmore (now VML) I worked on a pitch to redesign the Kellogg careers website. My idea was to personalize the corporate experience. High energy, colorful and friendly. By showcasing images of actual employees on the homepage and having them double as secondary navigation to keys sections, we would create an inviting environment for current and future employees. Utilizing color schemes and fonts from the Kellogg branding, I redesigned the page to include similar content as before with some re-prioritization.

GIFSOME

Roles: Concept, Wireframe & Design

Animated GIFs are taking the internet by storm and I was frustrated with the inability to easily send them through iMessages on my iPhone. The process is sometimes mysterious and lengthy and near impossible for your less tech-savvy friends. I wanted an easier way. Bouncing ideas off of a co-worker at lunch, I wire-framed Gifsome on a napkin and started design shortly after. The app would tie in with Giphy's api and use the same organization that they'd come up with. The app would be simple, click and hold to preview animation and a single tap would copy the image to your clipboard so you can paste it in a message quickly.

MAKE IT

Roles: Concept, Design & Proposal

Co-leading a team of coworkers while at Biggs | Gilmore (now VML) we were tasked with thinking of a new way to use an existing Kellogg project. Our concept was to introduce a DIY mentality to the cereal isle. We would Allow the consumer to create their own piece of art on a blank box and share it with their social spaces, using a specially created app. Kellogg would then select several winning designs at the end of the promotion and mass produce those designs on special edition boxes. Our idea won an internal competition, and selected to be presented at Kellogg's open pitch day in 2013.

FRANKLIN POSTERS

Roles: Concept & Design

The concept for these posters was to visualize our companies core values. My initial idea was to create posters that incorporated different historical figures or characters from pop-culture to visually explain these values. Starting with the value "Make it better" and having read Benjamin Franklin's auto-biography the year prior, I knew he was the perfect representation of this value. During his life he was an apprentice printer, a statesman, scientist, inventor, postmaster, & swim instructor, just to name a few. In everything he did, he continually worked to make it better in one way or another.

Heinz Redesign

Roles: Wireframe & Design

While at the agency Biggs | Gilmore (now VML) I was tasked with redesigning the Heinz 57 homepage. Given requirements and hierarchy by a UX team member, I started sketching out my own wireframes. One of the client goals was to include push their featured recipes using vivd imagery. Running with this idea I designed a page that would envelope the user in an 'experience'. I wanted to make an emotional connection to the user using imagery of recipes and landscapes that would change with the season. These scenes would stimulate the users and convey the feeling of what it's like to be in that setting or cook the featured dishes.
